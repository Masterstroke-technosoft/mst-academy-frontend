02 · Real-World Introduction Story

The ₹2 Crore Mistake - How a Great dApp Died Before It Launched

📖 Story

In December 2023, a group of four talented developers from Bengaluru built an incredible dApp on MST - a decentralised freelance marketplace where Indian gig workers could receive payments in stablecoins with zero platform fees. They called it KaamFi. The smart contracts were audited, the frontend was polished, and they had 2,000 beta testers on the MST testnet. They were ready to launch on mainnet and raise a seed round.


Then the first investor meeting happened. The VC asked: "What is your legal entity? Who owns the IP? What is the token utility - is it a security? How is the team equity split?" Silence. The four developers had never incorporated. They had no operating agreement. They had minted a governance token without any legal memo on its classification. The code was flawless. The business was nonexistent.


Six months later, the team disbanded. Two developers joined a US-based protocol. One took a job at a Web2 fintech. The fourth - the smart contract lead - is still sitting on unaudited contracts, waiting for the "right time" to relaunch. KaamFi never launched. Not because the product failed - it never got a chance. The foundation was missing.


This sub-module exists so that you - the MasterStroke graduate - never become the KaamFi story. Code is only 30% of a Web3 startup. The other 70% is legal structuring, token economics, and team alignment - the building blocks we will unpack right now.

Every hour spent on these blocks before writing a single line of Solidity is worth 100 hours saved later. The MST Grant Committee does not fund "great ideas." They fund great teams with sound legal and economic foundations. This sub-module teaches you how to build that foundation.

03 · The Three Building Blocks

Legal, Tokenomics & Team - The Startup Trinity

⚖️
Block #1: Legal Structure
Defines ownership, liability, IP protection, and compliance - the invisible shield around your code. Without it, you are personally liable for every smart contract bug.
🪙
Block #2: Tokenomics
The economic model that aligns incentives between users, developers, investors, and the protocol. A well-designed token economy creates network effects; a poorly designed one creates a speculative bubble.
👥
Block #3: Team Formation
The human layer. VCs invest in teams, not ideas. A balanced founding group with complementary skills, clear equity splits, and a vesting schedule is non-negotiable.
🗝️ The Golden Rule of Web3 Startups

You cannot fix a broken foundation after launch. Legal mistakes are permanent. Token mistakes are expensive (re-deployment, governance chaos). Team disputes kill startups faster than any competitor. Get all three right before you deploy to mainnet.

04 · Building Block 1

Legal Wrappers - Which Entity Should Your dApp Wear?

In Web3, the phrase "code is law" is often cited. But the real world has real laws, and ignoring them does not make them disappear. The legal entity you choose determines who can be sued, how you pay taxes, who owns the IP, and whether your token is a security.

Most early-stage MST projects should start as a Private Limited Company (India) or LLC (US) - you can transition to a DAO wrapper later as governance matures.
💡 Indian Founder Note

If you are based in India, the most common path is a Private Limited Company registered under the Companies Act, 2013. It offers limited liability, clear equity division, and the ability to accept foreign investment (including MST grants). A sole proprietorship is simpler but exposes your personal assets. Many Indian Web3 startups incorporate in Singapore or Delaware (US) for better crypto regulatory clarity - but the cost is higher. For the MST initGrant, an Indian Pvt Ltd is fully acceptable.

Key Legal Considerations for Your Entity

1
IP Assignment
Every line of code, every design asset, and the project name must be legally assigned to the company - not to individual founders. Without an IP assignment agreement, a departing founder can take the entire codebase with them. This is the #1 startup killer. Use a standard Founder IP Assignment Agreement signed by all co-founders at incorporation.
2
Token Legal Opinion
Before launching a token, you must determine whether it is a utility token or a security under the laws of your jurisdiction and the jurisdictions of your users. In India, there is no clear legislation yet, but the law is evolving. In the US, the Howey Test applies. A legal opinion from a Web3-focused law firm costs ₹50,000-₹2,00,000 but can save you from a ₹2 crore penalty later. MST grants include mentorship that can guide you through this.
3
Terms of Service & Privacy Policy
Even a fully decentralised dApp needs a frontend. That frontend collects data (wallet addresses, IPs). A privacy policy is legally required under GDPR (if you have EU users) and India's DPDP Act 2023. Draft one before launch. Do not copy-paste a template without understanding it. A wrong clause can expose you to liability.
4
Banking & Fiat On/Off Ramp
Your company needs a bank account to receive fiat investments, pay salaries, and pay for infrastructure. Many Indian banks are cautious about crypto businesses. Open a current account with a crypto-friendly bank (like ICICI, HDFC in some cases) or a neo-bank. Clearly document all crypto-to-fiat conversions for tax compliance.

05 · Building Block 2

Tokenomics - The Economic Engine of Your dApp

A token is not just a fundraising tool. It is the incentive mechanism that aligns every participant - users, developers, validators, and investors - towards a common goal. Poor tokenomics can destroy a perfectly engineered protocol. Good tokenomics can turn a modest product into an ecosystem.

🏪 Analogy: The Village Fair Economy

Imagine a village fair. The organisers print "Fair Tokens" - the only currency accepted at stalls. Stalls accept only these tokens. Visitors buy tokens at the gate. The organisers use the fiat collected to pay stall owners, maintain the fair, and invest in next year's event. If they print too many tokens, stalls lose value. If they print too few, visitors can't spend. If the token has no use beyond the fair, nobody wants it. Tokenomics is designing the rules of this fair economy - supply, distribution, utility, and sinks.

The 5 Pillars of Tokenomics Design

S
Supply - Total, Circulating, Max
Total Supply: all tokens ever created. Max Supply: hard cap (like Bitcoin's 21M). Circulating Supply: tokens currently in the market. Inflationary tokens (like ETH before EIP-1559) have no max supply. Deflationary tokens burn a portion of fees. Decide your model early - it is nearly impossible to change later without community backlash.
A
Allocation - Who Gets What?
The most scrutinised slide in any pitch deck. Typical buckets: Team & Advisors (15-20%, 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff), Investors (10-20%), Community & Ecosystem (30-50%), Treasury/Foundation (10-20%), Public Sale (5-10%). The exact numbers depend on your product. Rule: never give the team more than 25%. Investors want to see aligned incentives, not a founder's get-rich-quick scheme.
U
Utility - What Does the Token Actually Do?
A token with no utility is a meme coin - and meme coins rarely last. Utility must be intrinsic to the product: governance voting, staking for protocol fees, paying gas (like MSTC for gas on MST), accessing premium features, or serving as collateral. The more concrete the utility, the stronger the token's value proposition. If you can't explain the utility in one sentence, you have a problem.
V
Vesting & Lock-Ups - Aligning Long-Term Incentives
Team and investor tokens must vest over time - typically 4 years with a 1-year cliff (nothing unlocks for the first 12 months, then monthly linearly). This prevents founders from dumping tokens on day one. Smart contracts must enforce vesting - not just a promise. OpenZeppelin's VestingWallet contract is your friend. For MST projects, the grant program may require specific vesting terms to protect the community.
S
Sinks & Burns - Where Does Value Accrue?
A token must have deflationary pressure or value accrual mechanisms. Examples: a percentage of protocol fees used to buy back and burn tokens (like Binance's BNB burn). Staking locks up supply, reducing circulating supply. Protocol fees distributed to token stakers (like ve-tokenomics). Without sinks, the token is just a speculative instrument with no natural demand beyond "number go up."

⚠️ Security Token Warning

If your token represents a share of revenue, profit, or equity, it is likely a security token and must comply with securities laws. Do not guess. Get a legal opinion. The MST ecosystem encourages utility tokens with clear, non-security use cases. For RWA tokenization (Module 11), securities law compliance is mandatory - never skip this step.

06 · Building Block 3

Team Formation - The Only Thing VCs Really Care About

Ideas are abundant; execution is scarce. Every investor - from the MST Grant Committee to Sequoia - will tell you they invest in teams, not ideas. A stellar team can pivot a weak idea into a unicorn. A dysfunctional team will fail even with a perfect product. The founding team is the single most important building block.

Equity Split & Vesting - The Hardest Conversation You Must Have

Many startups die not because of market failure, but because of co-founder disputes. The equity split discussion must happen before a single line of code is written. No "we will figure it out later." Later never works.

✅ Do This
  • Have the equity discussion on day one, in writing.
  • Use a dynamic equity split if contributions are unequal (Slicing Pie model).
  • Implement a 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff for all founders.
  • Sign a Founder Agreement covering IP, roles, and departure terms.
  • Leave 10-15% unallocated for future key hires (ESOP pool).
❌ Don't Do This
  • Equal split "because we are friends" without considering contribution.
  • No vesting - a co-founder leaves after 3 months, keeps 33%.
  • Verbal agreements - "I trust you bro" is not a legal contract.
  • One founder owns 90% - no co-founder will be motivated.
  • Bringing in a "business guy" with 50% equity and zero crypto knowledge.
🗝️ The MST Grant Requirement

The MST Grant Committee explicitly asks about the founding team in the application. They want to see clear roles, vesting schedules, and proof that the team has been working together for at least 3-6 months. A solo founder with no collaborators is a red flag. If you are solo, find a co-founder during Phase 3 (Capstone) before applying for the grant.

Month 0
Idea + Co-founder Search. Validate the problem. Find a co-founder who complements your skills. Sign a mutual NDA if needed.
Month 1-2
Incorporation. Register the legal entity. Sign the Founder Agreement with IP assignment and vesting. Open a bank account.
Month 2-4
Prototype + Tokenomics Draft. Build the MVP on MST testnet. Draft the tokenomics. Get a legal opinion on token classification.
Month 4-6
Apply for MST initGrant. Submit the proposal with capstone as proof of concept. Show legal entity, tokenomics, and team vesting.

08 · Assessment Preparation

Top Facts - Know These Cold

01
Legal Entity First
Never deploy a mainnet dApp without a registered legal entity. In India, a Private Limited Company is the most common structure. Without it, founders are personally liable for all actions of the protocol.
02
IP Assignment is Non-Negotiable
All code, designs, and project names must be legally assigned to the company. A departing founder without an IP assignment can take the entire codebase - this has happened repeatedly in Web3.
03
Tokenomics Must Be Defined Before Deployment
Changing a token's supply model after launch is nearly impossible without a hard fork. Allocations, vesting, utility, and sinks must be designed and documented before the first mint.
04
The Minimum Viable Team is Three Roles
Visionary (CEO), Builder (CTO), and Marketer (Biz Dev). Solo founders almost never succeed in Web3. VCs and grant committees look for this triad.
05
Vesting Protects Everyone
Standard vesting: 4-year with 1-year cliff. No tokens for the first year, then monthly unlock. This prevents team members from leaving early with a large allocation.

Which of the following is the most recommended legal entity for an Indian Web3 startup aiming to raise funds and limit personal liability?
  • A Sole Proprietorship

  • D Personal bank account
✅ Correct Answer: B - Explanation

A Private Limited Company offers limited liability, allows equity structuring (ESOPs, investment rounds), and is acceptable for MST grants. Sole proprietorships expose personal assets. Unregistered partnerships lack legal clarity.

Q2
MCQ Easy 1 mark
What is the primary purpose of a Founder IP Assignment Agreement?
  • A To register the company with the ROC
  • B To legally transfer all code and design assets from individual founders to the company ✅
  • C To define the token utility
  • D To open a bank account for the startup
✅ Correct Answer: B - Explanation

Without an IP assignment, the code remains the personal property of the founder who wrote it. If that founder leaves, the company loses the right to use the code - a fatal blow.

Q3
MCQ Easy 1 mark
In tokenomics, what is a "vesting cliff"?
  • A The total maximum supply of a token
  • B A period (usually 1 year) before any team tokens begin to unlock ✅
  • C The moment a token is listed on a DEX
  • D A mechanism to burn tokens
✅ Correct Answer: B - Explanation

A vesting cliff means no tokens are released until a specified time (commonly 12 months). After the cliff, tokens unlock gradually (monthly) over the remaining vesting period.

Q4
MCQ Medium 1 mark
Which of the following is considered a "sink" mechanism in tokenomics?
  • A Minting new tokens for liquidity providers
  • B Using a portion of protocol fees to buy back and burn tokens ✅
  • C Airdropping tokens to early users
  • D Increasing the total supply through inflation
✅ Correct Answer: B - Explanation

A sink removes tokens from circulation, creating deflationary pressure. Buy-back-and-burn is a classic sink. Minting and airdrops increase supply - they are sources, not sinks.

Q5
MCQ Medium 1 mark
According to the "Golden Triangle" for Web3 founding teams, which three roles are considered essential?
  • A 3 Solidity developers, 1 designer, 1 community manager
  • B Visionary (Product), Builder (Tech), Marketer (Business) ✅
  • C CEO, CFO, COO
  • D Smart contract auditor, frontend dev, token economist
✅ Correct Answer: B - Explanation

While deep technical skills are crucial, without someone to define the product vision and someone to drive user adoption, a startup cannot succeed. The three roles must be complementary.

Q6
MCQ Medium 1 mark
Why do Web3 investors and grant programs (like MST) specifically look for a "vesting schedule" in the team allocation?
  • A To ensure the token price pumps on day one
  • B To align long-term incentives and prevent immediate dumping by founders ✅
  • C To increase the total supply slowly
  • D To comply with Indian labor laws
✅ Correct Answer: B - Explanation

Vesting ensures founders stay committed to the project for years, not days. Without it, a founder could sell all their tokens immediately after launch and abandon the protocol.

09 · Glossary

Key Terms - Sub-Module 19.1

Private Limited Company
A legal entity registered under the Indian Companies Act, 2013. Offers limited liability, separate legal identity, and the ability to raise equity funding.
IP Assignment Agreement
A legal contract signed by all founders that transfers ownership of all intellectual property (code, designs, brand) from the individual to the company.
Vesting
The process by which tokens or equity allocated to founders and employees unlock gradually over time, typically over 4 years with a 1-year cliff.
Cliff
An initial period (usually 12 months) during which no tokens or equity are released. If a founder leaves before the cliff, they receive nothing.
Token Utility
The specific function a token performs within a protocol - governance, staking, gas payment, access gating, or collateral.
Sink (Tokenomics)
A mechanism that removes tokens from circulation, creating deflationary pressure. Examples: buy-back-and-burn, staking lock, or fee burning.
Golden Triangle (Team)
The three essential co-founder roles in a Web3 startup: Visionary (Product/CEO), Builder (CTO/Lead Dev), and Marketer (Biz Dev/Growth).
Token Legal Opinion
A written analysis by a qualified attorney stating whether a token is likely a security under relevant laws, and recommending a regulatory path.
MST Grant Program
A funding track (initGrant up to $1,000, buildGrant up to $10,000, scaleGrant up to $50,000) for projects building on MST blockchain, requiring a solid team, tokenomics, and legal foundation.
ESOP Pool
A reserved percentage of company equity (usually 10-15%) set aside for future key employees as stock options - essential for hiring talent after funding.
