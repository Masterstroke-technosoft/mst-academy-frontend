02 · Real-World Introduction Story

The $2 Million Seed Round That Started with a $10,000 Grant

📖 Story

In early 2025, a two-person team from Indore - Priya (smart contract dev) and Arjun (growth) - built ChitFundFi, a decentralised chit fund protocol on MST. They received the MST buildGrant ($10,000) in March. By August, they had 3,000 active users on mainnet, a token launch, and a growing community. But $10,000 was running out.


Instead of applying for another grant, they decided to aim higher. Priya had heard of Y Combinator but never thought they stood a chance. “We're just two people from a tier-3 city. YC is for Stanford grads,” she thought. But their MST grant mentor encouraged them: “The MST grant is your validation. You have real users, real revenue. That’s more than 90% of YC applicants.”


They applied to Y Combinator’s S2025 batch. In the application, they listed “MST buildGrant recipient - 3,000 mainnet users” as their top traction metric. They referenced the MST ecosystem, the low-gas advantage, and the WASMify oracle integration. Three weeks later, they received an interview invitation. Two weeks after that, they were in. YC’s $500,000 investment gave them 18 months of runway. Nine months later, they closed a $2 million seed round led by a top Web3 VC.


The MST grant did not make them successful. It made them visible. This sub-module shows you how to use that visibility to unlock capital far beyond the MST ecosystem.

Grants are a launchpad, not a destination. The real journey begins when you take your validated product and traction to the global venture capital market. This sub-module is your guide to navigating that world - from warm intros to term sheets.

04 · Grant as Traction

Why VCs Love MST Grant Recipients

External investors see an MST grant as a signal of quality. It means your project has passed a technical and business review, has a working testnet/mainnet deployment, and is aligned with a growing ecosystem.

🗝️ The Three Things VCs Infer from Your MST Grant

1. Validation: Someone else already believed in you and gave you money - the hardest cold start problem is partially solved.
2. Technical Credibility: MST’s review process includes smart contract checks. VCs assume your code is not a complete disaster.
3. Ecosystem Lock-in: You’re building on a specific L1 with unique features (SARAL, WASMify). If the ecosystem grows, you benefit disproportionately - a venture-style bet.

In your pitch to external VCs, always lead with the MST grant in your traction slide. Example: “MST buildGrant recipient - $10,000 awarded. 3,000 mainnet users, $120K volume, 4.8/5 community rating.” This frames you as a vetted, post-grant startup, not a raw idea.

05 · Y Combinator

Y Combinator & Web3 - How to Get In

Y Combinator (YC) is the world’s most prestigious startup accelerator. It has funded 5,000+ companies including Stripe, Airbnb, Coinbase, and OpenSea. Its standard deal: $500,000 for 7% equity (as of 2025). For Web3 startups, YC is chain-agnostic but values traction and technical defensibility.

1
The YC Application - What Matters
The YC application is a 20-question form. Key questions: “What are you building?” (one sentence), “Who are your competitors, and what do you understand that they don’t?”, and “How far along are you?” For an MST project, you’d answer the last one with: “We’ve received an MST buildGrant, deployed on mainnet, and have 3,000 users.” That’s enough traction to stand out. YC also asks for a 1-minute video - same pitch structure as 19.2.
2
YC Interview - 10 Minutes to Convince
The interview is fast and intense. Expect rapid-fire questions on your product, market, and team. Use the same Q&A framework from 19.3. They will ask: “Why blockchain?” Have a crisp answer tied to MST’s low gas and SARAL. “Why now?” - tie to MST’s mainnet launch and growing ecosystem. Bring your MST grant certificate and a live demo (with fallback).
3
YC's Web3 Track Record
YC has backed Coinbase (W12), OpenSea (W18), Worldcoin, Protocol Labs (Filecoin), and dozens of DeFi/NFT projects. They understand tokens and tokenomics. Your MST grant helps because it proves you can ship on a real blockchain, not just write a whitepaper.

07 · Relationship Building

How to Get a Warm Intro to Any Web3 VC

1
Leverage Your MST Mentor & Network
Your MST grant mentor is a direct line to the ecosystem. Ask them: “Do you know any VCs who would be interested in our traction?” MST team members often have connections at top funds. A warm intro from the MST Foundation carries weight.
2
Twitter/X - The Web3 VC’s Living Room
Most Web3 VCs are active on X. Follow them, engage with their posts (thoughtfully, not spam), and share your MST milestones publicly. When you’re ready, DM them: “We’re an MST buildGrant project with 3K users and growing 20% MoM. Would love 15 minutes to share what we’re building.” Keep it under 280 characters - respect their time.
3
Attend & Speak at MST Events (and Hackathons)
MST hackathons, demo days, and community calls are where VCs scout for talent. Volunteer to present your project. Winning an MST hackathon or being featured in a community showcase puts you directly in front of investors.
4
Use AngelList, LinkedIn, and Warm Intros
Platforms like AngelList (WellFound) have Web3 syndicates. LinkedIn is less crypto-native but still useful for finding VCs. A warm intro from a fellow founder (even a non-Web3 one) is gold. Always ask: “Who do you know who invests in blockchain?”
08 · Pitching VCs vs Grants

Key Differences Between Pitching to MST Grants and Pitching to VCs

Aspect 🎓 MST Grant Pitch 💰 VC Pitch
Audience Ecosystem builders; care about MST alignment Financial investors; care about return on investment
Emphasis Technical innovation, MST feature usage Market size, traction, scalable business model
Tokenomics Utility, alignment with MST ecosystem Value accrual, token distribution, lock-ups
Team Capability, MST commitment Experience, ability to execute at scale
Ask Specific MST grant track ($X, deliverables) Equity/token round, valuation, use of funds
Follow-Up Grant report, milestone updates Investor updates, board meetings, future rounds

For VC pitches, you must add valuation, equity/token structure, and a detailed financial model. The MST grant deck is an excellent starting point, but you’ll need a few extra slides: a cap table, a competitive landscape (Web2 and Web3), and a detailed go-to-market plan.

09 · Common Mistakes

The Top 5 Mistakes Founders Make When Moving from Grant to VC

1
Relying Only on the Grant as Traction
VCs want to see user growth, revenue, or clear demand beyond the grant. Use the grant money to generate independent traction before approaching VCs.
2
Pitching “Blockchain” Instead of “Solution”
VCs invest in businesses, not technologies. Say “We help freelancers keep more of their earnings,” not “We’re a decentralised freelance protocol on MST.”
3
Not Having a Clear Token/Equity Structure
VCs will ask: “Is this an equity round or a token round? What’s the valuation? What are the lock-ups?” Have answers ready. Ambiguity kills deals.
4
Ignoring Legal & Regulatory Readiness
Before taking VC money, you need a clean legal entity, IP assignment, token legal opinion, and a data room with all documents. (Covered in 19.1.)
5
Not Timing the Ask
Approach VCs when you have momentum - a new grant award, a spike in users, a feature launch. A “fundraising update” with good news gets replies. A cold email with no news gets ignored.

12 · Glossary

Key Terms - Sub-Module 19.4

Y Combinator (YC)
A top startup accelerator that invests $500,000 for 7% equity in early-stage companies, including many Web3 unicorns.
Warm Introduction
A referral from a trusted mutual contact to an investor, dramatically increasing the chance of a meeting.
a16z crypto
The dedicated crypto arm of Andreessen Horowitz, one of the largest and most influential Web3 venture capital firms.
Data Room
A secure folder containing all key startup documents (pitch deck, legal, cap table, financials) shared with serious investors.
Cap Table
A spreadsheet showing the equity ownership of a company, including founders, investors, and option pools.
Seed Round
The first significant round of venture funding (typically $1M-$3M) to scale product and team after initial traction.
Alliance DAO
A leading Web3 accelerator and founder community, known for its deep crypto expertise and selective cohort.
Social Proof
Evidence that others have validated your product or team - in Web3, a grant, audit, or partnership serves as social proof.
