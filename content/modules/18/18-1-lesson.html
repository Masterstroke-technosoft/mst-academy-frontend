02   Real-World Introduction Story

From Idea to Impact - How the Grant Tracks Fuel a Project

📖 Story

Priya, a full-stack developer in Bengaluru, had been freelancing on Web2 projects for years. She saw how local artists were losing 40% of their earnings to platform commissions. One evening, she sketched out a concept for "KalaChain" - a decentralised marketplace where artists mint their work as NFTs and receive instant payments when a fan buys a print, with zero platform cut.

Priya had the idea and a rough Figma prototype, but no funding to quit freelancing and build it. A friend told her about the MST initGrant. She applied with a two-page concept note and a screen recording of her prototype. Within two weeks, she received $1,000 - enough to cover her living expenses for a month while she built a working smart contract and a basic React frontend on the MST testnet.

With a testnet-ready MVP in hand, she applied for the buildGrant. This time the application was more rigorous: she submitted her GitHub repository, unit test coverage report, and a short demo video. The MST grants committee reviewed her code, provided architecture feedback, and approved $10,000. The funds covered a professional security audit (arranged through MST's partner network) and paid a UI designer to polish the interface.

After a successful mainnet launch with 200 beta artists onboard, Priya needed to scale. The scaleGrant provided $50,000 for a targeted marketing campaign, liquidity mining incentives to bootstrap the marketplace's token economy, and legal consultation to structure the project as a proper entity. Within six months, KalaChain had 5,000 artists and was processing over $2M in monthly volume - all built on MST, all without giving away equity.

Priya's journey is not a fantasy. It is the exact path the MST grant program is designed to support. By splitting funding into three carefully sized tiers, MST reduces the risk for both the foundation and the builder - ensuring money is deployed only when real progress is demonstrated, while never taking ownership of your project.

03   Background & Philosophy

Why MST Built a Tiered Grant System

Blockchain ecosystems grow through network effects: more developers → more dApps → more users → more value locked → more developers. The bottleneck is often the very first step: a talented developer with a strong idea but no capital to stop freelancing and build. Traditional venture capital demands equity, traction, and warm introductions - inaccessible to the vast majority of global builders.

MST's grant program is designed as a public good funding mechanism. It is not an investment arm seeking returns; it is a growth engine that allocates a portion of the MST treasury to projects that increase the utility and user base of the network. The tiered structure solves three problems simultaneously:

1
De-risking early-stage bets
At the idea stage, there is no code, no users, no proof. Giving a large grant at this point would be irresponsible. By capping initGrant at $1,000, MST can fund hundreds of experiments cheaply. Most will fail - and that's expected. The ones that show promise graduate to the next tier.
2
Progressive commitment
Each tier requires the builder to demonstrate increasing levels of dedication and skill. An initGrant requires only a concept note. A buildGrant requires a working testnet deployment. A scaleGrant requires mainnet users and traction metrics. This ensures the grant committee's time is spent evaluating serious proposals, not filtering spam.
3
Non-monetary support at the right time
An early-stage prototype doesn't need a professional audit or a marketing agency. A mainnet application does. The grant tracks bundle mentorship, security reviews, GTM strategy, and legal support exactly when the project needs them - not before, and not as an afterthought.
🗝️ Equity-Free by Design

None of the MST grant tracks take equity, tokens, or revenue share from the project. The foundation's return is ecosystem growth: every successful dApp brings more users, more transactions, and more demand for MST the asset. You retain 100% ownership of your code, your company, and your future.

05   Deep Dive

Track 1: initGrant - The Idea Spark (up to $1,000)

The initGrant is the entry point. It is designed for the "napkin stage" - when you have a clear problem statement, a proposed blockchain-based solution, and perhaps some mockups or a proof-of-concept, but no deployed code. The grant recognises that the single biggest barrier at this stage is time - the time you would otherwise spend on freelance projects or a day job to pay bills.

$1,000
Maximum Grant Amount
2 - 3 weeks
Typical Decision Time
Concept + Mockup
Minimum Application Requirement

What initGrant Covers

💡
Living stipend
The $1,000 is unrestricted - you can use it for rent, food, internet, or tools. The only expectation is that you build what you proposed. There is no requirement to quit your job, but many recipients use the funds to carve out focused building time.
🛠️
MST developer tooling credits
Free testnet MST tokens for gas, priority access to MST SDK documentation, and a dedicated Discord channel for initGrant recipients to ask technical questions.
📋
Milestone review
At the end of the grant period (typically 4 - 6 weeks), you submit a short report and a testnet deployment. The MST team reviews it and provides feedback - this review is your entry ticket to apply for a buildGrant.

Application Requirements

Applying for an initGrant is deliberately simple. The grants committee knows you don't have a product yet. They evaluate the clarity of thought and the feasibility on MST.

P
PROBLEM
What real-world problem are you solving?
A concise statement. "Artists lose 30 - 40% to platform fees" is better than "Web3 for creators." Be specific. Include who suffers, how much, and why existing solutions fail.
S
SOLUTION
How does blockchain - specifically MST - solve it?
Explain why a traditional database or a Web2 platform cannot solve this. Reference specific MST features if applicable (low gas, EVM compatibility, SARAL onboarding, WASMify for real-world data).
M
MOCKUP
Show us the vision - even if it's a Figma screenshot.
A link to a Figma prototype, a PDF with wireframes, or even a hand-drawn sketch photographed and embedded in the application. The committee needs to visualise what you intend to build.
Y
YOU
Why are you the right person to build this?
Link to your GitHub, LinkedIn, or portfolio. If you have no prior blockchain experience, mention your Web2 development background and your commitment to learn Solidity/MST SDK during the grant period.
💡 initGrant Success Tip

The committee sees many applications with vague aspirations like "decentralised Twitter." The ones that stand out solve a concrete, narrow problem and explain exactly which smart contracts they will write. "A registry contract for land records + a frontend for gram panchayat officers" beats "a blockchain for governance."

Track 2: buildGrant - From Testnet to Production-Ready (up to $10,000)

The buildGrant is for projects that have moved beyond the idea phase and have a functional testnet deployment. The committee now expects to see smart contracts that compile, a frontend that connects to a wallet (BridgeKey), and basic unit tests. This track is about transforming a rough MVP into a polished, secure, and user-ready product.

Maximum Grant Amount
3 - 5 weeks

Testnet MVP + Tests

What buildGrant Provides

🔒
Professional security audit
MST has partnered with leading audit firms (and internal security engineers) to provide a subsidised or fully covered audit of your smart contracts. The audit cost alone can exceed $10,000 on the open market. The buildGrant ensures your code is reviewed by professionals before it touches real user funds.
🏗️
Architecture deep-dive with MST core engineers
A 1-on-1 (or small group) session where you present your contract architecture. MST engineers will suggest optimisations, flag potential scaling bottlenecks, and ensure you are using the right MST protocols (SARAL for onboarding, WASMify if you need off-chain data, etc.).
🎨
UI/UX budget
A portion of the grant is earmarked for design. You can hire a freelance designer, purchase a premium UI kit, or pay for user testing. The committee understands that a great smart contract behind a confusing interface will never gain traction.
📊
Milestone-based payouts
The $10,000 is not disbursed upfront. It is split into 2 - 3 milestones, each tied to a concrete deliverable (e.g., audit report addressed, frontend deployed to a staging environment, mainnet launch candidate ready). This protects both you and the foundation.

Application Requirements for buildGrant

The bar is significantly higher. You must demonstrate technical execution.

Requirement What the Committee Looks For Common Rejection Reasons

⚠️ Common Misunderstanding

The buildGrant is not a prize for building a testnet app. It is a funding agreement to turn that testnet app into a production-grade dApp. If your application essentially says "I built it, give me money," it will be rejected. The committee invests in the future work, not the past work.

Track 3: scaleGrant - Go Big on Mainnet (up to $50,000)

The scaleGrant is the pinnacle of the MST grant program. It is reserved for projects that have successfully launched on MST mainnet, have real users, and are ready to scale. At this stage, the question is no longer "does this work?" but "how do we get this into the hands of thousands or millions of users?"

What scaleGrant Provides

The scaleGrant is a comprehensive growth package. It goes far beyond cash, bundling services that would cost $100,000+ if purchased independently.

📈
User acquisition & marketing budget
Funds allocated for targeted campaigns, content creation, community management, and listing on dApp directories. MST's marketing team may also feature your project in official channels (Twitter, blog, newsletter), providing a direct pipeline to the existing MST community.
💧
Liquidity & token incentives
If your project involves a token, the scaleGrant can fund liquidity pools on MST-native DEXs, yield farming campaigns, or airdrop distributions to bootstrap the economic flywheel. MST's tokenomics advisors will help design sustainable incentive structures that avoid the "farm-and-dump" death spiral.
⚖️
Legal entity setup & compliance
Through MST's legal partners, you receive guidance on incorporating a company (Singapore, Delaware, or Indian entity), drafting terms of service and privacy policies, and ensuring your token complies with relevant regulations. This is critical if you plan to raise VC funding later.
🤝
BD introductions & partnership facilitation
The MST ecosystem team will introduce you to potential partners - other dApps for composability, wallet providers, enterprise clients exploring MST, and even VCs from the MST investor network. These warm introductions can accelerate your growth more than any marketing spend.
🛡️
Ongoing security monitoring
Access to real-time monitoring tools that watch your deployed contracts for anomalous activity, and a retainer with an audit firm for any emergency fixes or upgrades.

The scaleGrant Selection Process

Unlike initGrant and buildGrant, which are evaluated primarily on documentation, the scaleGrant process includes a live interview with the grants committee. You will present your project, answer questions about your metrics, and defend your growth plan. The committee is looking for:

T
TRACTION
Real, verifiable on-chain usage.
The committee will check your contract on MST Explorer. They expect to see genuine transaction volume, not just you and your friends testing. 100 active users is better than 10,000 bot accounts.
R
RETENTION
Do users come back?
A one-time airdrop that caused a spike and then flatlined is not traction. The committee looks for repeat usage patterns - weekly active users, recurring transactions, organic referrals.
U
UNIT ECONOMICS
Can this become self-sustaining?
Even if currently burning grant money, you need a plausible path to sustainability. Transaction fees, premium features, token appreciation - whatever your model, it must be defensible.
E
ECOSYSTEM VALUE
How does this project make MST stronger?
Projects that attract new users to MST, provide infrastructure other dApps can use, or bring real-world assets on-chain are prioritised over a standalone app that could exist on any chain.

Think of the grant tracks like a motorsport development program. initGrant is a karting scholarship - you have raw talent and a dream; we give you a kart and a track to prove yourself. buildGrant is a Formula 3 seat - you've shown you can race; now we give you a proper car, engineers, and a pit crew to compete professionally. scaleGrant is Formula 1 - you have a winning car and a fan base; we provide the sponsorships, the media exposure, and the global circuit to make you a world champion. 10   Assessment Preparation

Top Facts - Know These Cold

01
The Three Tracks and Their Caps
initGrant: up to $1,000 (concept / early prototype). buildGrant: up to $10,000 (testnet-ready product). scaleGrant: up to $50,000 (mainnet live with users). None take equity or tokens.
02
Entry Requirements
initGrant requires only a concept note and mockup - no code. buildGrant requires a working testnet deployment with unit tests. scaleGrant requires live mainnet usage and verifiable traction.
03
Progressive, Not Parallel
You cannot apply for a buildGrant without having completed an initGrant (or demonstrating equivalent progress independently). The tracks are a ladder, not a menu. Each step builds on the evidence of the previous one.
04
Non-Monetary Support
buildGrant includes a subsidised/free security audit and architecture review. scaleGrant includes marketing, liquidity incentives, legal entity setup, and BD introductions. These bundled services often exceed the cash value of the grant itself.
05
Common Rejection Traps
Applying for a track that doesn't match your stage (e.g., seeking buildGrant with only an idea); no clear milestone plan; no demonstrable MST-specific usage; requesting equity-like terms. The committee funds builders, not speculators.
11   Glossary

Key Terms - Sub-Module 18.1

initGrant
The first MST grant tier, providing up to $1,000 for projects at the concept stage. Requires only a problem statement, solution outline, and mockup. No code is needed.
buildGrant
The second MST grant tier, providing up to $10,000 for testnet-ready projects. Includes security audit, architecture review, and UI/UX budget. Requires working testnet deployment and unit tests.
scaleGrant
The third and highest MST grant tier, providing up to $50,000 for mainnet-live dApps with real users. Includes marketing, liquidity incentives, legal entity setup, and business development introductions.
Equity-Free Grant
A funding model where the grantor provides capital without taking ownership (shares, tokens, or revenue share) in the recipient's project. All three MST grant tracks are equity-free.
Milestone-Based Payout
A disbursement structure where grant funds are released in tranches only after the builder demonstrates completion of agreed-upon deliverables, reducing risk for both parties.
Testnet-Ready
A project state where smart contracts are deployed on a blockchain testnet (not mainnet), unit tests pass, and a basic frontend exists. The minimum entry point for buildGrant.
Grants Committee
The group of MST foundation members, engineers, and ecosystem leaders who review grant applications, conduct interviews, and approve or reject proposals.
Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy
A plan outlining how a product will reach its target users, including marketing channels, pricing, partnerships, and launch timeline - a key support area in scaleGrant.
