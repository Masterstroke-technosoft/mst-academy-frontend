02   Real-World Introduction Story

Three Developers, Three Paths - Where They Are After 2 Years

📖 Story

In 2022, three friends - Meera, Karan, and Sameer - completed the same blockchain bootcamp in Bangalore. They all knew Solidity, Hardhat, React, and could deploy a basic DeFi dApp. Two years later, their careers look completely different.


Meera chose the startup path. She joined a 7-person DeFi protocol as a Smart Contract Engineer. The pay was ₹18 LPA - decent, but she received 1.5% equity in tokens. For 18 months, she worked 50-hour weeks, shipped a lending protocol, survived a bear market, and helped raise a Series A. Today she's the Lead Engineer at the same startup, earning ₹65 LPA, and her tokens vest over 4 years at a projected value of ₹2 Cr+. She loves the ownership and impact, but it was intense.


Karan went the agency route. He didn't want to bet on a single project. He joined a Web3 development agency that builds dApps for multiple clients - mostly DeFi protocols and NFT marketplaces. He works on 3-4 projects per year, touches many codebases, and has become a generalist across Ethereum, Polygon, and MST. His salary is steady at ₹25 LPA, with no equity but low stress. He's now the Solidity Lead and gets freelance offers at $100/hour. Variety and stability define his choice.


Sameer took the founder's leap. Right after the bootcamp, he applied for an MST initGrant with a SARAL-based mobile payment app idea. He received $1,000 and mentorship. He spent 6 months building the MVP on testnet, then won a buildGrant of $10,000. By month 18, he had a team of 4, a working product on MST mainnet, and was pitching VCs. Today his startup has raised $750k in a seed round. He pays himself ₹2 LPA to conserve runway - high risk, high potential. He owns 60% of the company.


Same skills. Three different outcomes. The difference wasn't technical ability - it was the career track each chose, aligned with their personality, risk tolerance, and life goals. This sub-module helps you find your own Meera-Karan-Sameer decision.

Your career is not just about what you can build - it's about what you want your life to look like. Web3 offers unprecedented flexibility: you can earn a Silicon Valley salary from a tier-2 Indian city, own equity in a protocol that could 100x, or build your own company with grant funding. But each path demands different trade-offs, and making an informed choice is the most important career decision you'll make after acquiring the skills.

03   Background & History

How Web3 Careers Evolved from “Bitcoin Miner” to Diverse Ecosystem

2009 - 2015
The solo contributor era. Most crypto work was done by individuals: mining, running nodes, contributing to Bitcoin Core. The only “career path” was being a core developer for an open-source project - unpaid and highly technical.
2016 - 2018
The ICO boom and the birth of Web3 startups. Ethereum's smart contracts enabled crowdfunding. Thousands of startups raised millions via token sales. This created the first proper “Web3 startup employee” roles - Solidity devs, dApp frontend engineers, and community managers were suddenly in demand. Many projects failed, but the template was set.
2019 - 2021
DeFi summer and the rise of protocol treasuries. Protocols like Uniswap, Aave, and Compound became billion-dollar entities. They could hire full-time teams paid from protocol fees or treasuries. The “protocol core contributor” path emerged - work directly for a DAO or foundation, often with token-based compensation.
2022 - Present
The diversification of talent paths. Today, a blockchain developer can choose: (1) join a venture-funded startup, (2) work for an agency serving multiple clients, (3) become a core contributor to a major L1/L2 protocol, (4) go independent as a freelancer or security auditor, (5) launch a grant-funded project, or (6) join a DAO as a contributor. The MST ecosystem specifically incentivises the founder path through its grant programme, while also hosting a job board for agencies and startups building on MST.

05   Technical Deep Dive

Deep Analysis - Compensation, Skills, and Who Thrives in Each Track

1
Startup Employee - The Accelerated Learning Machine
Who it's for: You want to see a product go from 0 to 1, you enjoy wearing multiple hats, and you are comfortable with ambiguity and long hours.
Typical roles: Smart Contract Engineer, Full-Stack dApp Developer, Product Manager, DevRel.
Compensation breakdown: Base salary (₹12-40 LPA in India for junior-senior) + token allocation (typically 0.5-3% for early engineers, vested over 4 years). Tokens may be illiquid for years.
Key skills developed: Ownership mentality, rapid prototyping, understanding of tokenomics and go-to-market, cross-functional collaboration.
How to get in: Network at hackathons, contribute to the startup's open-source code, be active in their Discord, apply through MST job board or Web3 job platforms like Web3.career, CryptocurrencyJobs, and LinkedIn (with Web3 keywords).
Red flags to watch: Unclear tokenomics, no product-market fit, founder without technical background, no legal entity. Always verify the team on LinkedIn and GitHub.
2
Agency / Freelance - The Swiss Army Knife
Who it's for: You value flexibility, variety, and steady income. You enjoy jumping between projects and technologies. You may be a digital nomad or prefer a 9-5 with clear boundaries.
Typical roles: Solidity Developer, Frontend dApp Specialist, Security Auditor (freelance), Technical Writer.
Compensation: Agencies pay ₹10-30 LPA in India; freelance rates range from $50-$150/hour depending on expertise. No equity. Some agencies offer profit-sharing.
Key skills developed: Breadth across multiple L1s/L2s, client management, estimation, writing clean, maintainable code for handover.
How to get in: Build a strong GitHub portfolio with 3-5 diverse projects. Apply to Web3-focused agencies (ConsenSys Mesh, OpenZeppelin, Chainstack, and local Indian agencies). For freelance, start on platforms like Gitcoin, SuperteamDAO bounties, and Bountycaster, then move to direct client relationships.
Pro tip: Specialise in an ecosystem (like MST) to become the go-to agency expert. A developer who knows SARAL and WASMify can command premium rates because the talent pool is tiny.
3
Protocol Core Contributor - The Deep Tech Specialist
Who it's for: You love infrastructure, consensus algorithms, cryptography, or developer tools. You want to work on problems that affect millions of users at the protocol level. You don't mind a slower pace and a more academic environment.
Typical roles: Core Protocol Engineer (Rust/Go), Cryptography Researcher, DevRel (ecosystem), Security Engineer.
Compensation: Base salary competitive with top tech companies (₹30-80 LPA in India for senior roles; $150k-$250k globally). Often includes a significant token allocation (e.g., 0.1%-0.5% of network tokens).
Key skills: Deep understanding of the protocol's consensus mechanism, networking layer, VM, and governance. Ability to write clear technical specifications and participate in open-source communities.
How to get in: Contribute to the protocol's open-source repositories for 3-6 months before applying. Engage in governance forums. Attend core dev calls. For MST, look for openings at MST Foundation, partner infrastructure teams, or ecosystem funds. The MST DevRel team often scouts active contributors from Discord and GitHub.
Example path: Start by answering questions on Discord → write a forum tutorial → submit a PR fixing a bug → get invited to a contributor call → apply for a full-time role.
4
Founder / Grantee - The Visionary's Gamble
Who it's for: You have a burning idea that keeps you up at night. You can tolerate extreme uncertainty and are willing to earn little or nothing for 1-2 years in exchange for equity and impact. You can sell - to users, investors, and early employees.
The MST Grant Advantage: MST's tiered grant system is uniquely suited to de-risk the founder path. initGrant ($1,000) validates the idea. buildGrant ($10,000) funds testnet development. scaleGrant ($50,000) funds mainnet launch and initial user acquisition. This is non-dilutive funding - you keep 100% equity.
Key skills: Beyond coding: storytelling, tokenomics design, community building, fundraising, legal structuring.
Compensation: Founders typically pay themselves ₹2-6 LPA initially (or nothing), focusing on grants and equity. The upside is ownership of 60-100% of a potentially high-value venture.
How to start: Apply for MST initGrant with a clear problem statement, solution, and a 3-month deliverable. Use the programme's mentorship to refine your MVP. Win buildGrant to hire a co-founder or contractor. ScaleGrant opens doors to VCs. Even if your startup fails, the grant experience is a massive CV boost for the other three tracks.
Risk mitigation: Many founders keep a part-time freelance income (Track 2) for the first 6-12 months. Others save 12-18 months of living expenses before jumping full-time.
W
RISK
Which track has the highest financial risk?
Founder (Track 4) - you may earn nothing for 1-2 years. Next is startup employee (equity may be worthless). Agency and protocol roles are more stable.
W
REWARD
Which track has the highest financial upside?
Founder - uncapped. Startup employee can also see life-changing equity if the company succeeds. Protocol contributors with early token allocations can also do extremely well. Agency income is capped by hourly rates.
H
HOW
How do I decide which track fits me?
Assess your risk tolerance, need for stability, desire for ownership, and preferred working style. The assessment question at the end of this sub-module will force you to make that decision concretely.

09   Assessment Preparation

Before You Write Your Roadmap - Key Principles

1
Clarity on track choice
Your roadmap must name the specific track you are committing to for the next 2 years. Saying “I'm flexible” is not acceptable. Choose one, with reasoning.
2
Measurable milestones
Every 6-month interval must have a concrete deliverable: a deployed contract, a job title, a grant amount, a revenue figure, a follower count, etc.
3
MST ecosystem leverage points
Identify exactly how you will use MST's grants, Discord, DevRel support, hackathons, and tools (SARAL, WASMify) to accelerate your path.
4
Risk acknowledgment and mitigation
Acknowledge the biggest risk of your chosen track (e.g., startup fails, grant not renewed) and explain your backup plan.
5
Realism
A plan that says “I will be a millionaire in 6 months” will score zero. A plan that shows understanding of the time it takes to build skills, reputation, and products will score high.
10   Glossary

Key Terms - Sub-Module 20.5

Token Equity
Ownership in a protocol represented by tokens (often ERC-20), typically vested over 4 years. Can become highly liquid and valuable, but may also be worthless if the project fails.
Core Contributor
A developer, researcher, or community member who works directly on a blockchain protocol's infrastructure, often funded by a foundation or DAO treasury.
MST Grant Program
A tiered funding initiative (initGrant $1k, buildGrant $10k, scaleGrant $50k) that provides non-dilutive capital and mentorship to builders in the MST ecosystem.
Vesting
A schedule that releases tokens or equity over time (e.g., 4 years with a 1-year cliff), aligning long-term incentives between founders, employees, and investors.
Bootstrapping
Building a startup without external investment, using personal savings or revenue. Common in the founder path before grants or VC funding.
DAO (Decentralised Autonomous Organisation)
An organisation governed by smart contracts and token-holder voting. Many protocols operate as DAOs, offering contributor grants and bounties instead of traditional employment.
