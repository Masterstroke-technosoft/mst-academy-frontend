02   Real-World Introduction Story

The Tester Who Became a Protocol Engineer in 9 Months

📖 Story

In 2022, Ananya was a manual QA tester at a Bengaluru IT services firm. She had never written a smart contract, never used MetaMask, and thought blockchain was “just Bitcoin.” Her salary was ₹4.8 LPA, and her work was repetitive.


She joined a structured blockchain programme - exactly like this one. Within 3 months she had deployed an ERC-20 token on testnet. By month 6 she had written a simple AMM from scratch and understood gas optimisation. Month 9 she applied to a mid-sized DeFi protocol as a junior Smart Contract Engineer. The interview included a live coding challenge - writing a staking contract - and she nailed it.


Today Ananya earns ₹22 LPA working remotely for a protocol with a global team. She audits contracts in Solidity, writes Hardhat scripts, and reviews pull requests for the core protocol. Her QA background actually helps - she thinks in edge cases, which makes her excellent at security reviews.


Ananya's career jump happened because she matched her skills (attention to detail, testing mindset) with the right Web3 role - not just “blockchain developer,” but specifically Smart Contract Engineer. That precise matching is what this sub-module teaches.

The Web3 job market is incredibly diverse. Unlike traditional tech where you have broadly “frontend,” “backend,” and “full-stack,” blockchain introduces entirely new roles that didn't exist five years ago - and many of them are not about coding at all. Understanding this landscape is the single most important career decision you'll make.

03   Background & History

How Web3 Jobs Evolved - From Bitcoin Era to the Multi-Chain World

2009 - 2013
The Solo Miner Era. Jobs barely existed. A handful of cypherpunks and C++ developers contributed to Bitcoin Core. Most “work” was mining or running full nodes as a hobby. No formal roles, no salaries - just passion and ideology.
2015 - 2017
Ethereum Creates the Smart Contract Developer. With Solidity and the EVM, a new role was born: the smart contract engineer. Startups like ConsenSys began hiring Solidity devs. ICO mania in 2017 created a gold rush for “blockchain developers,” though many projects were vaporware. The first proper job titles emerged: dApp Developer, Solidity Engineer, Blockchain Architect.
2018 - 2020
The Bear Market Professionalisation. The hype died, but serious infrastructure was built. Roles diversified: Protocol Researcher (formal verification, cryptoeconomics), DevRel (developer relations, education), Security Auditor (specialised firms like Trail of Bits). India saw its first cohort of blockchain startups - Polygon (then Matic), WazirX, CoinDCX - creating local jobs.
2021 - 2023
DeFi Summer & the Multi-Chain Explosion. Every L1 (Solana, Avalanche, Fantom) and L2 (Arbitrum, Optimism) needed its own ecosystem developers. New roles emerged: Subgraph Developer (The Graph), Data Analyst (Dune Analytics wizards), Community Manager (Discord-native). Salaries in India for solid Solidity devs crossed ₹25 LPA.
2024 - Present
Enterprise & AI Integration. With RWA tokenisation, post-quantum security, and on-chain AI agents, roles have again expanded. Today, a Web3 career is as stable and varied as any tech sector - but requires specific knowledge of consensus, wallets, and decentralised infrastructure.
Your Start
Right now, the market is hungry for developers who deeply understand one ecosystem - like MST. General blockchain devs are common. Specialists who can deploy on MST, use SARAL, and write WASMify modules are rare and well-paid.

🗝️ The Single Most Important Concept

You do NOT need to be a Layer 1 engineer to have a great career. Many Web3 professionals work in Layers 3 and 4, earn excellent salaries, and never write a line of Solidity. The key is to match your existing skills with the right layer, then add blockchain knowledge. A brilliant React developer can become a dApp Frontend Developer in weeks - not years.

05   Technical Deep Dive

Every Role, Deconstructed - Skills, Tools, Salaries, Day-to-Day

For each role, we'll cover: what they actually do, essential tools and languages, salary benchmarks (India and US/Remote), and a typical day. Pay attention - the assessment matches roles to these descriptions.

01
Smart Contract Engineer (Solidity / Vyper / Rust)
What you do: Write, test, audit, and deploy smart contracts. You build the on-chain logic for tokens, DeFi protocols, DAOs, and NFTs. You understand gas optimisation, storage patterns, and security vulnerabilities intimately.
Languages: Solidity (primary), Vyper (Ethereum), Rust (Solana, Near, WASMify), Cadence (Flow).
Tools: Hardhat, Foundry, Remix, OpenZeppelin, Slither, Echidna.
Indian Salary: Entry ₹8-15 LPA, Mid ₹20-40 LPA, Senior ₹50-80 LPA+ (at top protocols).
Global Remote: $80k-$200k+ depending on experience.
Day-to-day: Review a new EIP, write a staking contract with 100% test coverage, debug a reentrancy issue found by an auditor, deploy to testnet, gas-optimise a loop.
02
Protocol Researcher / Cryptoeconomist
What you do: Design consensus algorithms, tokenomics, and cryptographic primitives. You write whitepapers, simulate economic attacks, and prove security properties. This is the most academic role - often requiring a PhD or equivalent self-study.
Skills: Advanced cryptography (ZK, MPC, threshold signatures), mechanism design, game theory, formal verification.
Tools: Python (simulations), LaTeX, SageMath, Rust, Lean/Coq for proofs.
Salary: Typically higher than smart contract engineers; often $120k-$250k globally. In India, ₹50 LPA+ for experienced researchers.
Day: Model a new slashing condition for Trika POSA, write a simulation of validator game theory, review latest academic paper on ZK-STARKs.
03
Security Auditor / Formal Verification Engineer
What you do: Audit smart contracts and protocols for vulnerabilities. You think like a hacker - reentrancy, overflow, front-running, storage collision. Formal verification engineers write mathematical proofs that contracts behave correctly.
Tools: Slither, Mythril, Echidna, Certora Prover, Manticore, manual code review.
Entry path: Often come from smart contract engineering or cybersecurity background.
Salary: Premium over smart contract devs; India ₹20-50 LPA, global $100k-$200k.
Day: Review a DeFi lending contract, find a read-only reentrancy, write a PoC exploit, produce audit report. Formal verification: write a Certora rule ensuring “total supply never exceeds cap.”
04
dApp Frontend Developer
What you do: Build the user interface for Web3 applications. You connect wallets, sign transactions, display on-chain data, and handle loading states. You are a React/Next.js developer who deeply understands ethers.js, Wagmi, and WalletConnect.
Languages: TypeScript, React, Next.js, Tailwind CSS.
Web3 Tools: ethers.js / viem, Wagmi, RainbowKit, WalletConnect, The Graph (queries).
Indian Salary: ₹6-20 LPA entry/mid, ₹25-40 LPA senior.
Day: Implement a “Connect Wallet” flow with BridgeKey, build a token swap UI, handle transaction pending states, fetch user's NFT collection from a subgraph.
05
Full-Stack dApp Engineer
What you do: Own both smart contracts and the frontend. You design the architecture, write the Solidity contracts, deploy them, and build the React app that interacts with them. This is the most versatile - and in-demand - role in startups.
Stack: Solidity + Hardhat + TypeScript + React + Graph Protocol (subgraphs).
Salary: India ₹12-35 LPA, global $80k-$150k.
Day: Add a new governance feature: write the Governor contract, test it, deploy, then build the voting UI, all in one sprint.
06
Subgraph / Indexer Developer
What you do: Write subgraphs that index on-chain events into a queryable database. Frontends and dashboards rely on you to provide fast access to historical blockchain data.
Tools: The Graph (AssemblyScript), Goldsky, Subsquid, Dune (SQL).
Skills: Understanding event schemas, GraphQL, efficient indexing patterns.
Salary: India ₹8-20 LPA, global $70k-$120k.
07
Developer Relations (DevRel) / Technical Evangelist
What you do: Bridge the gap between the protocol and its developer community. You write tutorials, give talks at conferences, create demo dApps, answer questions on Discord, and gather developer feedback for the product team.
Skills: Strong communication, technical enough to build demos (Solidity basics + frontend), public speaking, writing.
Salary: India ₹10-30 LPA, global $80k-$150k. Often includes tokens.
Day: Write a tutorial “Build Your First MST dApp in 15 Minutes,” present at ETHIndia, answer questions on MST Dev Forum, record a YouTube walkthrough.
08
Community Manager
What you do: Manage and grow the protocol's community on Discord, Telegram, and Twitter. Run AMAs, organise community calls, moderate discussions, and be the friendly face of the project. This role rarely requires coding - but deep product knowledge is essential.
Salary: India ₹4-12 LPA (entry), ₹15-25 LPA (experienced at major protocols). Often token incentives.
Day: Host weekly community call, respond to 50+ Discord messages, coordinate a meme contest, escalate a bug report to the dev team.
09
On-Chain Data Analyst / Dune Wizard
What you do: Analyze blockchain data using SQL and visualisation tools. You answer questions like: “What's the retention rate of our NFT holders?” or “What's the impermanent loss across all Uniswap v3 pools?” You build public dashboards that become industry standards.
Tools: Dune Analytics, Flipside Crypto, Nansen, Python (for complex stats).
Salary: India ₹8-25 LPA, global $70k-$130k.
10
Web3 Product Manager
What you do: Define the product vision, prioritise features, manage the roadmap, and coordinate between engineering, design, and marketing. You need to deeply understand user needs in a decentralised context - which often differ radically from Web2.
Skills: Tokenomics design, understanding of governance, UX for non-custodial wallets, stakeholder management.
Salary: India ₹15-40 LPA, global $90k-$160k.

10   Glossary

Key Terms - Sub-Module 20.1

Smart Contract Engineer
Developer who writes, tests, and deploys on-chain code (Solidity, Rust). Responsible for gas optimisation, security, and integrating with frontends.
Protocol Researcher
Designs consensus algorithms, tokenomics, and cryptographic mechanisms. Often publishes academic-style papers and runs simulations.
Security Auditor
Reviews smart contracts for vulnerabilities using manual analysis and automated tools (Slither, Mythril). Produces audit reports for protocols.
dApp Frontend Developer
Builds the user interface for Web3 apps. Connects wallets, signs transactions, and displays on-chain data using React and ethers.js.
Full-Stack dApp Engineer
Owns both smart contracts and the frontend. Versatile role common in startups - handles Solidity + React + subgraph integration.
Subgraph Developer
Writes indexing logic (AssemblyScript/GraphQL) to make on-chain events queryable for fast frontend use.
DevRel (Developer Relations)
Technical evangelist who educates developers through tutorials, talks, and demos. Bridges protocol engineering with its developer community.
Community Manager
Manages protocol's social channels (Discord, Telegram), organises events, and moderates discussions. Focus on engagement and product knowledge.
On-Chain Data Analyst
Uses SQL on Dune/Flipside to analyse blockchain data. Builds dashboards tracking TVL, user behaviour, and protocol metrics.
Web3 Product Manager
Defines product strategy for Web3 apps, coordinates engineering and design, understands tokenomics and wallet UX.
